The First Seven Judges of Ma'at

Usekh-nemmt #1

From Iunu (City of the Pillar)

"I have not transgressed the universal law."

The principle of Staying Within the Bounds. To "commit sin" is to step outside the ordained path, to break the harmony of Ma'at.

Hept-khet #2

From Kher-aha (Place of the Combat)

"I have not violated the sanctity of another's being."

Honoring the sacred vessel of life. "Robbery with violence" is a double crime against property and the inviolable magical integrity (hekau) of a person.

Fenti #3

From Khemenu (City of the Eight)

"I have not misappropriated what was not born of my own ka."

Governs true ownership from the Ogdoad, the primordial source. To "steal" is to take that which your spirit did not generate or earn.

Am-khaibit #4

From the Cavern of Becoming

"I have not extinguished the divine spark before its appointed time."

The finality that only the divine may command. To "slay" is to usurp the role of the gods in the cycle of life, death, and renewal.

Neha-her #5

From the Place of the Mouth of Caves

"I have not taken the life-force of the land without reciprocity."

Grain is the transformed flesh of Asar (Osiris). To steal it insults the cycle of death and regeneration that feeds the nation.

Ruruti #6

The Twin Guardians of the Horizon

"I have not diverted sustenance meant for the akh of the divine or the dead."

Offerings are the currency between worlds. To pilfer them severs the connection and starves the gods and ancestors.

Arfi-em-khet #7

From the Place of the Jackal

"I have not violated the sacred trust of the temple."

Temple goods are the god's physical power (sekhem). This theft is a direct assault on the divine presence in the community.

üîÆ Updated Weekly Exercises

Exercise 1: Daybreak Declaration to Usekh-nemmt

Each rising: "Today I take the Extended Stride, walking within universal law. I shall not transgress the ordained path of Ma'at."

Exercise 2: Evening Examination with the 7 Judges

Each evening, review your day against each Judge's principle. Ask: "Did I honor the sanctity of others? Did I take only what my ka generated?" Record with compassion.

Exercise 3: Sacred Boundary Meditation

Daily 20-minute meditation visualizing yourself walking the Extended Stride within golden boundaries, honoring each Judge's domain in your thoughts, words, and actions.

Exercise 4: Offerings of Reciprocity

Create a small daily offering (grain, water, or symbolic item) with the declaration: "I take only with reciprocity, honoring the cycle of Neha-her."
